Synthwave? What is that?

Synthwave is a sub-genre of electronic music that is inspired in the synth music and pop culture of the 1980s. The musical aspect features instrumental tracks with a "retro futuristic" theme and is characterized by the use of retro synths. This genre draws its inspiration from various genres that originated in the 80s like Synth Pop, Progressive Electronic, Italo-Disco and Electro-Disco.

In addition to its musical elements, synthwave places great importance on the visual aspect. Album covers are heavily influenced by 80s films, particularly sci-fi, as well as TV shows such as Miami Vice and video games, all key elements of 1980s culture.

Welcome to the retro future!

This is where the past and the future collide and like we said, it goes beyond the music because it’s also known for its visual aspect. The genre's imagery draws heavily from the futuristic neon aesthetic of the 1980s, creating a unique style.

One of the most recognizable elements is the use of bright neon colors, which evoke a sense of nostalgia for the neon lights, and are used to create stunning artwork depicting sunsets and neon lights of bustling cities. Retrowave also incorporates other elements of 80s pop culture, including movies, TV shows, and video games. The genre's artwork features pixelated arcade game art or scenes from classic films.

Another defining element is its use of geometric shapes and patterns, which give the artwork a futuristic, high-tech feel, and are used to create complex visual landscapes.
Overall, retrowave's visual aspect is as important as the music in capturing the spirit of the 80s, so whether you're a longtime fan or a newcomer to the world of retrowave, there's something for everyone.
